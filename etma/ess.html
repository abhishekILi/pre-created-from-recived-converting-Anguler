4. Installation Checks
Ser Reference Checks Remarks
4.1 - Portable Transformers.
4.1.1  Check the location/ quantity of portable transformer as per approved drawing.
4.1.2  Check the storage arrangement of cables (as per diagram) near portable fuse board.
4.2 - Portable Connection Panel.
4.2.1  Check the availability of portable emergency connection panel with associated length of flexible cable.
4.2.2  Check the location and storage arrangement of panel as per approved drawings.
4.2.3  Check for proper securing with bulkhead.
4.3 - Cable Storage.
4.3.1  Check flexible cables are for storage at designated locations.
4.3.2  Check it only one (upper) bracket is fitted and the lower bracket is omitted (if length of cable is ≤4m)
4.3.3  Check it only one set of cable is stowed on any one bracket unless specifically approved.
4.3.4  Check the portable fuse board cables are stowed using only single bracket stowage.
4.4 - Cable Route.  Check the route of cables for all equipment provided with emergency supply system (as per drawing).
4.5 - Tally Marking/ Identification.  Check it flexible cables are properly tallied as per BR2170 V-II chapter 37, clause 3725.
4.6 - Location of Talley.
4.6.1  Check tally marking is fitted preferably above the upper bracket of the stowage.
4.6.2  Check it the PVC covers are properly fitted on all emergency cables stowage.
4.7 - 4.7.1  Inspect the condition of cable for proper securing, damage (if any).
4.7.2  Check the accessibility of cable.
4.8 - Emergency Connection Boxes (Record Results in Table 6.1).
4.8.1  Check it all ECBs are provided with embossed tally fitted on the bulkhead immediately adjacent.
4.8.2  Check the identification tally of each ECB is placed.
4.8.3  Check the ECBs are fitted as high as possible consistent with either being within reach of a man of average height.
4.8.4  Check the proper earthing of ECBs.
4.8.5  Check it all types of ECBs/ connection points are covered with rubber covers to prevent from rusting/ moisture accumulation/ dust.
4.9 - Permanent Cables (Record Results in Table 6.2).
4.9.1  Check the insulation resistance of the Emergency connection boxes/ permanent &
Flexible cables (insulation value should be ≥ 20 MΩ between each phase and earth.
4.9.2  If two ECBs are connected then insulation should be ≥ 10 MΩ)
4.9.3  Check the continuity of cables.
4.9.4  Check the phase rotation of cables.
4.10 - Flexible Cables (Record Results in Table 6.3).
4.10.1  Check the flexible cables associated with the ESCB's, important fuse panels and emergency terminals of the main SWBDs are
long enough to reach the farthest emergency connection box in the respective compartment.
4.10.2  Check the length of cable as per approved drawing.
4.10.3  Check the continuity of cables.
4.11 - Tools.
4.11.1  Check the availability of insulated key in securing clip provided on the box.
FOST Advisory 4.11.2  Provision of insulated 'S' hooks for securing of emergency cables.
4.12 - Supply Breaker.  Check the capacity of emergency supply breaker (should be ≥ 250A).
