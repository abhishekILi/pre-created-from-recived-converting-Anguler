BROAD GUIDELINES FOR SYSTEM OP-CHECKS
(I) Power Supply/ Power Supply cards failure
Ser Checks Status
(Sat/ unsat)		 Remarks
1 In the ESM rack, air filters of the ESM units - E/J Processor (WOR), C/D Band Processor - present at the Front Panel are required to be cleaned by removing the front panel air filter screws. Remove the air filter and clean the same using vacuum cleaner/ air gun or shake the dust off the filter using hand. After cleaning it, place the air filters back to its location and refit the air filter cover with its screws.
2 An UPS of 10 KVA rating is recommended for the whole system (to power up the ESM racks) to ensure smooth system functioning and to isolate the ship power supply fluctuations/ transients/ abrupt power switching.
3 Check whether the blowers fixed at the rear of the processor units ESM E/J Processor (WOR), C/D Band Processor are in running condition.
4 Whenever system is switched OFF and needs to be switched ON again, DO NOT IMMEDIATELY SWITCH ON, and wait for 5 minute. A time gap of at least 5 minute has to be maintained between each switch OFF and switch ON.
(II) Degradation/ Failure in Front End Electronic components
Ser Checks Status
(Sat/ unsat)		 Remarks
1 Avoid exposure of system to high RF radiation when at harbor/ during Sea & Action of other near ships.
2 Ensure usage of limiters in the Gonio section (DFA) to reduce the future damage of the front end electronic components.
(III) Discontinuities/ poor contact in EW cables
Ser Checks Status
(Sat/ unsat)		 Remarks
1 Ensure that connectors (RF cables, Control Cables, Power Supply Cables) are intact at all units on mast. Ensure the same at ESM rack top.
2 Ensure that all the EW cables connectors and unused/ spare connectors on rack top are protected with water resistant tape/ vulcanizing tape to avoid corrosion.
3 Ensure all cables are properly routed on mast to avoid tread ON/ step ON/ crushing of the cables by ship staff during mast climbing for other maintenance works like painting/ cleaning etc.
4 Recommended to replace the Radial RF Cable of Radial make with RF cables of Times Microwave.
(IV) Interface issues (radar blanking, LAN connections, Gyro)
Ser Checks Status
(Sat/ unsat)		 Remarks
1 Ensure Gyro feed tapping point finalized during the system installation and HATs is not changed or broken. Also, ensure that ship Gyro data is reaching to the input of the interfacing unit by switching on SDN/ Gyro converters as configured for each platform.
2 Ensure the radar pre-trigger (Pre-Sync) output pulse is reaching the radar blanking junction box (Interface) of the EW System by co-ax cable from the available onboard radars. Make sure that the cable is not broken/ physically damaged/ disturbed. Pre-trigger should meet the following as per MIL 751 A:
  • Pulse Amplitude: 6-28 V across 75 ohm load (Positive Polarity)
  • Pre-trigger Delay: 1μsec (minimum) before Actual Transmission pulse
  • Pulse Width: 1 μsec (minimum)
(V) Failure in digital cards/ circuit
Ser Checks Status
(Sat/ unsat)		 Remarks
1 Check for any loose cables out of its position, PCBs Loosening. If found so, ensure proper tightening of loose cables, fix cables into their positions, insert PCBs firmly into their slot by pressing on the facial panel of the PCBs and tighten the PCB fixing screws softly (only 3 to 4 turns of the screw in the clockwise direction is sufficient). DO NOT over tighten the PCB fixing screws.
2 Inspect the following, wherever applicable:-
  • Position of LRU circuit breaker
  • Position of CYDA switch
  • LEDs in ESM rack
  • LEDs on processor LRUs
(VI) System checks (Installation, VME address, off-sets)
Ser Checks Status
(Sat/ unsat)		 Remarks
Checks Under System Power Off Condition:
1 Ensure all system units (ESM Rack, Console, DFS Unit, DFA (starboard), DFA (port), C/D Antenna Radome of mast mount units DFA End unit, Front End unit, Isolation transformer and operation console are properly installed as per the installation manual.
2 Ensure all the Antenna Radome of mast mount units DFA (starboard), DFA (port), C/D Antenna and DFS unit, Front End unit, 18-40 RDF units (if installed) are free from dust and foreign deposits/ bodies. These units need to be cleaned with soft damp cloth.
CAUTION: DO NOT PAINT RADOMES/ MAST MOUNT UNITS/ CABLES
3 For the DFS unit, clean the dust and foreign deposits/ bodies using air gun/ vacuum cleaner so that heat dissipation from the DFS unit happens properly.
4 To ensure that the UPS (Installed only for Operation Console) Batteries do not drain or become dry, switch ON the isolation transformer at least for one hour in a month (especially during ship refits), since the isolation transformer 230 V AC output supplies the power to UPS of Operation Console.
Checks Under System Power On Condition:
5 Carry out pinging checks (LAN Interconnectivity checks) from the Operation Console/ Computer with the following IP addresses by typing following commands in the command prompt of the Console, wherever applicable:

(a) For WOR connectivity:
ping 192.2.1.116 -t for Central PPG-2C0
ping 192.2.1.117 -t for TPP-2C17
ping 192.2.1.118 -t for PPG-MCM
ping 192.2.1.251 -t for TPP2C14

(b) For C/D connectivity:
ping 192.2.1.196 -t for PPG Card
ping 192.2.1.197 -t for TPP-2E29

With the above IP addresses, check for proper pinging/ reply. VME switch settings may be verified if pinging is not OK.
6 Ensure all OB Spares are available at all times and are functionally OK.
7 Power ON / maintenance BITE checks
  • Verification of BITE logs
  • Problems identified in BITE
  • DFS and DFA video levels according to BITE
8 Display of tracks in console containing frequency parameter, PW & PRI for available emissions in harbour
9 Are threshold level settings in WOR & CAS as per HATs/ SATs document
10 Are attenuation levels in C/D band as per HATs/ SATs document
11 Are offsets in DDT cards appropriate?
